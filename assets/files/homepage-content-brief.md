# Playbook Advisory Group — Homepage Content Brief

> **Purpose of this document**
> This brief consolidates the client's vision, raw messaging, strategic framing, and structural intent for the Playbook Advisory Group homepage. It is intended as a reference and direction-setting document for the content writer, who should use it to produce polished, on-brand copy for the final website.
>
> **Tone target:** Calm, intelligent, confident. Authoritative without being corporate. Closer to Oliver Wyman or Arup than a typical consultancy brochure site.
>
> **Key constraint:** All content must frame Playbook's work strictly as advisory and governance — not as project management, programme management, or design and construction consultancy. This is a contractual requirement until February 2027. Framing independence from delivery as a deliberate, strategic choice (not a limitation) is central to the positioning.

---

## Brand Positioning Line

**Clarity. Control. Confidence.**

This is the primary positioning line and should inform the rhythm and structure of the homepage copy throughout. Each of the three service areas maps to one of these words (see Section 5).

---

## 1. Hero Section

### Intent
The opening of the site should immediately establish what Playbook does, who it serves, and why it is different. It needs to feel definitive — not tentative or wordy. The client has provided the following core language to work from.

### Raw Client Copy

> **Clarity. Control. Confidence.**
>
> We strengthen the decisions that shape major programmes, projects and developments — and bring them under control when it matters most.
>
> Operating between board oversight and technical delivery, we remain independent from execution — ensuring decisions are clear, objective, and defensible.

### Content Writer Guidance
- The positioning line (**Clarity. Control. Confidence.**) should lead — it may sit above a headline or form the headline itself.
- The two supporting sentences should be refined into polished hero copy. They are close to final but may benefit from a light editorial pass for rhythm and impact.
- Avoid adjectives like "leading," "innovative," or "world-class." Confidence should come from precision of language, not superlatives.
- The phrase "independent from execution" is important — it should remain or be clearly echoed.

---

## 2. The Problem

### Intent
This section articulates why Playbook exists. It reframes the common assumption that programmes fail in delivery — arguing instead that failure is rooted in the decisions made before delivery begins.

### Raw Client Copy

> Major programmes rarely fail in delivery. They fail in the decisions that precede it.
>
> Unclear governance. Lack of clarity. Diffused accountability. Experience that never reaches the moment of decision.
>
> By the time issues become visible, risk is already embedded.
>
> We work with sponsors and senior leaders to bring structure, clarity, and control to the environments where those decisions are made.

### Content Writer Guidance
- This section should feel diagnostic and credible — like something a senior leader would immediately recognise from their own experience.
- The staccato rhythm of "Unclear governance. Lack of clarity. Diffused accountability." is intentional and should be preserved or echoed.
- The final sentence ("We work with sponsors and senior leaders...") is the pivot from problem to solution. It should feel like a natural, confident transition — not a sales pitch.

---

## 3. Where We Sit — And Why It Matters

### Intent
This section explains Playbook's positioning in the advisory landscape. It draws a clear distinction between delivery-side capability (abundant) and sponsor-side independent authority (often missing). This is the philosophical heart of the proposition.

### Raw Client Copy

> Delivery capability is abundant. What is often missing is independent sponsor-side authority — ensuring decisions are clear, governance is robust, and programmes are set up to succeed before delivery begins.
>
> Technical project managers and delivery teams play a critical role once programmes are defined and underway. Our role sits earlier — and alongside — that process.
>
> We work with sponsors to strengthen the authority, governance, and decision-making environment in which complex programmes are set up and delivered.

### Content Writer Guidance
- This section should feel like a smart reframe — not a criticism of delivery teams, but a clear articulation of a gap.
- The phrase "sits earlier — and alongside" is key to the positioning and should be preserved or refined rather than removed.
- The section heading ("Where we sit — and why it matters") is directional; the writer may refine this for the final site.

---

## 4. What We Are — and What We Are Not

### Intent
Playbook's independence from technical delivery is both a contractual necessity and a genuine differentiator. This section should turn that constraint into a statement of principle. The "We are / We are not" structure is intentional and should be preserved in some form.

### Raw Client Copy

**We are:**
- Sponsor-side advisory, aligned to client interests
- Specialists in governance and decision quality
- Engaged early to reduce risk and strengthen outcomes
- Independent from delivery and execution

**We are not:**
- A design or construction consultancy
- A delivery-led project management firm
- Embedded resource or capacity support

> By remaining independent from delivery, we ensure that decisions are clear, governance is robust, and programmes are set up to succeed before execution begins.

### Supporting Statements (for use within this section or adjacent copy)

> "We are intentionally independent from delivery."
>
> "Operating between board oversight and technical delivery, we remain independent from execution — ensuring decisions are clear, objective, and defensible."
>
> "Independent by design."
>
> "We are not a design or construction consultancy. Our role is to strengthen the environment that governs delivery — not to deliver it. This independence allows us to provide clear judgment, objective challenge, and advice that is aligned solely to the sponsor's interests."

### Content Writer Guidance
- The binary "We are / We are not" structure works well visually and rhetorically — preserve it.
- "Independent by design" is a strong, compact phrase that could function as a standalone callout, subheading, or section anchor.
- The closing paragraph ("By remaining independent from delivery...") ties the section together well. The writer should ensure it lands as a statement of principle, not a disclaimer.

## 5. When We Are Typically Engaged — Three Service Areas

### Intent
This section introduces the three core service areas, each linked to one of the brand positioning words (Clarity / Control / Confidence). It should give potential clients an immediate sense of where in their programme lifecycle Playbook is relevant.

### Service Area 1 — Before Commitment *(links to "Clarity")*

**Setting programmes up for success**

> Where a major scheme is being defined, we are engaged to bring structure and discipline to the early stages.
>
> We work with sponsors to scope complex programmes, strengthen governance, and ensure decisions are clear and aligned from the outset — maximising the likelihood of successful delivery.
>
> This establishes the clarity required for programmes to proceed with confidence.

### Service Area 2 — When Programmes Are Under Pressure *(links to "Control")*

**Restoring control to complex programmes and projects**

> Where a programme is drifting, unclear, or exposed, we are brought in to stabilise and reset direction.
>
> We quickly establish where issues sit, bring structure to decision-making, and define a clear recovery plan to regain control.
>
> This restores confidence that the programme is back on a stable and defensible footing.

### Service Area 3 — When Independent Assurance Is Required *(links to "Confidence")*

**Providing independent sponsor assurance**

> Where additional assurance is required — by sponsors, funders, or governance bodies — we are engaged to provide an independent, sponsor-side view of programme performance and decision integrity.
>
> We assess whether governance is functioning as intended, whether decisions remain aligned to the original strategy, and whether risks are being appropriately managed.
>
> This provides confidence that complex investments remain on track and are being delivered in a controlled and defensible way.

### Content Writer Guidance
- Each service area has a clear three-part structure: context → what we do → outcome. Preserve this rhythm.
- The closing sentence of each ("This establishes the clarity... / This restores confidence... / This provides confidence...") should echo the brand positioning words — writer may refine these to land more naturally.
- The headings ("Before commitment," "When programmes are under pressure," "When independent assurance is required") are directional. Final site headings may be refined.
- Visual treatment: the client envisages these three areas being visually distinct on the page — potentially cards, a stepped layout, or a tabbed structure. Copy should be written to work within a relatively compact format.

## 6. Bridge to the Diagnostic — The Capital Governance Diagnostic

### Intent
After introducing the three service areas, the copy should bridge to Playbook's diagnostic offer. This is a product/service entry point — the structured first step that applies regardless of which service area a client is entering from.

### Raw Client Copy

> **Every engagement begins with clarity.**
>
> Whether we are engaged to set up a programme, restore control, or provide independent assurance, the first step is always the same.
>
> We establish a clear, structured understanding of how decisions are being made, where risks sit, and where governance needs to strengthen.
>
> This is delivered through our **Capital Governance Diagnostic**, which provides the foundation to take control and move forward with confidence.
>
> A pre-condition for making safe, defensible decisions. It provides the clarity required to take control — and proceed with confidence.
>
> Because we are independent from technical delivery, the Diagnostic provides an objective sponsor-side health check of where governance and decision-making need to be strengthened — before delivery begins and significant commitments are made.

### Content Writer Guidance
- "Every engagement begins with clarity" is a strong anchor line and should be treated as a section headline or a prominent pull quote.
- The **Capital Governance Diagnostic** should be named explicitly and could link through to the Services page.
- The final sentence (beginning "Because we are independent...") reinforces the independence positioning — it should not be cut.
- Tone: this section should feel methodical and reassuring. A prospective client should feel that engaging Playbook is a structured, low-risk starting point.

## 7. Additional Context for the Content Writer

### Business Context
- Playbook Advisory Group is a newly established, founder-led consultancy based in Leamington Spa, with a presence in Birmingham's Jewellery Quarter.
- The founder has deep experience in capital programme governance — the website must convey institutional credibility even before the firm has a portfolio of publicly referenceable case studies.
- The target audience includes: NHS estates leads, local authority programme directors, university estates teams, senior sponsors in private sector development and infrastructure.

### Platform and Structure
- The homepage is one of eight planned pages. Others include: The Playbook Model, Services, Sectors, Insights, Case Studies (placeholder), About, and Contact.
- The homepage copy should function as the narrative entry point — introducing the firm, the problem it solves, and its approach — with each section naturally directing readers deeper into the site.

### What to Avoid
- Stock consultancy language: "delivering value," "end-to-end solutions," "holistic approach," "bespoke."
- Passive or hedged language. Playbook speaks with authority.
- Any reference to project management, programme management, or design and construction consultancy services.
- Overselling. The tone is calm and confident — not promotional.

### Visual Considerations (for coordination with design)
- The client is interested in a video or motion background for the hero section (content TBC).
- The three service areas (Section 5) should be visually differentiated — the copy should be written to sit within card or panel-style containers.
- Pull quotes and callout statements (e.g., "Independent by design," "Every engagement begins with clarity") should be flagged so the designer can treat them as typographic feature elements.
