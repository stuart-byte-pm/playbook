# Playbook Advisory Group — Brand Positioning & Strategy

---

## 1. Core Identity

**What do we want to be known for?**

> *"Clarity, control and confidence"*

- We unpick difficult problems and create clarity
- We strengthen the sponsor environment that directs delivery
- Clients can rely on us to take control of their problems and deliver certainty
- The go-to advisors who can sort out and lead complex projects
- We are a safe pair of hands
- We deliver **certainty**
- We get things done — high-output work ethic

**Known for:**
- Judgment and decision quality
- Objective oversight of our clients' projects and concerns
- Being a specialist extension of the client's leadership team
- Bringing structure and disciplined decision-making where complexity, competing priorities and regulatory scrutiny create ambiguity

**Programme Leadership Statement:**
> By providing senior-led, sponsor-side advisory and programme leadership at an early stage, Playbook seeks to strengthen decision quality, reduce avoidable downstream risk, and ensure that capital investment programmes enter delivery with clarity, control, and confidence — rather than reliance on late-stage intervention.

---

## 2. Positioning & Boundaries

**Where we sit in the market — and where we refuse to sit.**

- We operate at the space between Board oversight and programme execution — as an extension to the client's leadership team
- Most consultancies are delivery-led — we operate **upstream** from that
- We strengthen the sponsor environment that governs delivery
- We are **not** a delivery consultancy, not interim PMs, not 'bum-on-seats' capacity — we are specialist advisory
- Independence from execution is fundamental to credibility
- The brand must signal **authority**, not resource augmentation
- As well as being a confident advisory partner to scope and set up projects well, we want to be the **go-to advisors** if clients have a problem project — cutting through complexity to bring clarity and drive
- We are a safe pair of hands that resolves complex issues

**Core positioning statement:**
> Playbook will act exclusively for the client and protect their interests.

## 3. Priority Audience

### Primary Decision-Makers

- Director of Estates / Director of Strategy
- Capital Programme Managers
- NHS Trust CEOs / CFOs and Board Members
- Local Authority Directors, Senior Officers, SROs, Portfolio Holders
- Operations Directors
- Development Directors
- Developer Sponsors / Funders / Investors
- Developers looking to launch into an investment who need guidance

*Secondary:* Other large consultants who recognise the need for front-end expertise.

### Client Pain Points

- Reliance on delivery advice without assurance from a trusted, independent partner
- Lack of independent challenge
- Reputational exposure at project gateways
- Diffused accountability
- Escalating cost before commitment
- Political and audit scrutiny

### Influencers (Non-Decision-Makers)

- Programme Directors
- Finance Leads
- Internal PMOs
- External Cost Consultants

**They must feel:** reassured, protected, and better informed.

## 4. Differentiation & Competition

**Why Playbook is structurally different:**

- Delivery capability is abundant — **independent sponsor governance is scarce**
- We complement delivery consultancies — we do not compete with them
- We are senior-led, not leveraged delivery teams
- Our entry product (**Capital Governance Diagnostic**) is structured and repeatable — designed to get into a client quickly, understand and embed, outline the issues, and pave the way for an extended commission
- We price for **value and judgment**, not attendance

### Competitor Landscape

| Competitor Type | Characteristic | Our Position |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 Consultancies | Scale but embedded in delivery | We sit upstream |
| Mid-Tier PMs | Strong delivery but not structurally independent | We are structurally independent |
| Interims | Embedded resource, not governance authority | We provide governance authority |

> **We sit at the governance bridge.**

## 5. Brand Role in Work-Winning

**The brand functions as a:** trust accelerator, authority signal, and margin protector.

- Must immediately signal **board-level credibility**
- Must remove the "small consultancy" risk perception
- Must support **£850/day** positioning (mid-market rate)
- Must feel institutional, calm, disciplined
- Must strengthen proposals and Diagnostic credibility
- Must look like a **mature boutique from Day 1**

### Desired Emotional Response

> *"These people understand governance exposure."*
> *"This feels controlled and serious."*
> *"This is defensible advice."*
